SCORING RULES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

STEP 1 — Fill in pre-computed values (do not change these):
  breakdown.skill_match      = deterministicSkillScore
  breakdown.experience_match = experienceMatchScore

STEP 2 — Compute semantic_match (YOUR calculation, 0–100):
  This measures meaning-based alignment NOT already counted in skill_match.
  Consider:
    - Domain relevance: does the candidate's background match the industry/domain of the role?
    - Transferable competencies: does experience description demonstrate relevant practices?
    - Project relevance: do projects demonstrate applied use of job-relevant concepts?
    - Recency: is the most relevant experience recent, or is it from many years ago?
  Scoring guide:
    90–100: Candidate background is directly domain-aligned with strong, recent evidence
    70–89:  Candidate background is adjacent with clear transferable elements
    50–69:  Candidate background is partially relevant with limited direct evidence
    30–49:  Candidate background is weakly relevant, mostly indirect or outdated
    0–29:   No meaningful semantic alignment found
  STRICT: Do NOT count skills already in matched_skills again here.

STEP 3 — Compute formatting (YOUR calculation, 0–100):
  Assess the structural quality of the resume as a document:
    95:  All sections present (Summary, Skills, Experience, Projects/Education), dates consistent, clean hierarchy, bullet points start with strong action verbs.
    90:  Most sections present, minor inconsistencies, good use of action verbs.
    85:  Core sections present but sparse or inconsistent, some passive language.
    75:  Missing key sections, poor structure, lack of action verbs.
    60:  Severely unstructured, hard to parse, wall of text.
  STRICT: Score the resume as provided — do not assume ideal formatting.

STEP 4 — Compute penalty:
  penalty = missingSkills.length × 5
  Cap: penalty cannot make final_score below 0.

STEP 5 — Compute final_score using this EXACT formula:
  final_score = round( (skill_match × 0.40) + (experience_match × 0.30) + (semantic_match × 0.20) + (formatting × 0.10) - penalty )
  Then clamp: if final_score < 0 → set to 0. If final_score > 100 → set to 100.
  final_score MUST be a whole integer. No decimals.

WORKED EXAMPLE (for formula verification only — do not use these numbers):
  skill_match=80, experience_match=72, semantic_match=65, formatting=90, missing=2
  = round((80×0.4)+(72×0.3)+(65×0.2)+(90×0.1) - (2×5))
  = round(32 + 21.6 + 13 + 9 - 10)
  = round(65.6)
  = 66

STEP 6 — Compute confidence (YOUR calculation, 0–100):
  How certain are you that the ATS output reflects the true match quality?
  Scoring guide:
    90–99: All required skills are explicitly present in the resume text — no inference needed
    70–89: Most required skills are explicit; 1–2 required an inference or synonym match
    50–69: Several required skills required inference; experience dates were ambiguous
    30–49: Many skills inferred; resume was sparse or poorly structured
    0–29:  High uncertainty — resume text was too vague to score reliably

STEP 7 — Write summary (YOUR calculation, 1–2 sentences):
  A factual, neutral assessment of the match.
  Mention: the skill match percentage, whether experience requirement is met, and the most critical gap if any.
  Do NOT give advice. Do NOT say "you should". Be descriptive only.
  Example: "The candidate matches 3 of 4 required skills and meets the 2-year experience threshold, with REST APIs as the only missing required skill."
