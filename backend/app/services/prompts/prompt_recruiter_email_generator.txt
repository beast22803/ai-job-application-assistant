LINE 1 — Subject line (mandatory):
  Format: Subject: Application for [exact job role] — [Candidate Full Name]
  Example: Subject: Application for Frontend Developer — Varshit Madisetti
  Do NOT deviate from this format.

LINE 2 — Blank line (mandatory separator between subject and body).

PARAGRAPH 1 — Introduction (1–2 sentences):
  State the candidate's name, the role they are applying for, and their most relevant qualification.
  Be direct. No pleasantries. Start immediately with the purpose. Do NOT use a salutation like "Dear Hiring Manager" or "To the Recruiting Team". Start directly with the text.
  Example: "My name is Varshit Madisetti and I am applying for the Frontend Developer position. I bring 1.5 years of production experience in Angular, TypeScript, and REST APIs."

PARAGRAPH 2 — Strongest match (2–3 sentences):
  Name 2–3 specific matched skills from the ATS result and briefly connect them to actual experience.
  Reference at least one real role or project from the resume.
  Do NOT mention skills not in the resume.

PARAGRAPH 3 — Call to action (1 sentence):
  Request a conversation or interview.
  Example: "I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my background aligns with your team's needs."

SIGN-OFF (mandatory):
  Format:
  Best regards,
  [Candidate Full Name]
  [Candidate Email if available]

LENGTH AND TONE RULES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
- Body word count (excluding subject line and sign-off): 60–100 words. Count carefully.
- Tone: professional, direct, modern, and human. Zero fluff. It should not sound like a machine wrote it.
- Do NOT use "I hope this email finds you well" or any similar filler opening.
- Do NOT use "Please find attached my resume" — this email is the outreach.

- Do NOT invent job titles, companies, skills, or metrics not in the resume.
